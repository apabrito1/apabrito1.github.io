My notes on several Bend-and-Break lemmas.



This post documents a brief account of several bend-and-break theorems as I read about them. Let us fix the notational convention that we are going to use in these notes. All schemes are of finite type over a field $k.$ By a variety we mean a separated, integral scheme of finite type over a field. A curve is a 1-dimensional connceted, reduced proper scheme over a field $k$, not necessarily irreducible. By a curve on a variety $X$ we mean a nonconstant morphism from a curve with smooth irreducible components to $X$, or equivalently its image in $X$. 



First let me mention a few things about morphisms that contract curves. By the famous Stein Factorization theorem, any proper morphism $f:X\to Y$ between varieties can be factorised as $X \xrightarrow{f'} Y'\xrightarrow{g} Y$ where $f'$ has connected fibers and $g$ is a finite morphism.
