My notes on several Bend-and-Break lemmas.

This is a document that I am preparing as I read about various bend-and-break theorems. Let us fix the notational convention that we are going to use in these notes. All schemes are of finite type over a field $k.$ By a variety we mean a separated, integral scheme of finite type over a field. A curve is a 1-dimensional connceted, reduced proper scheme over a field $k$, not necessarily irreducible. By a curve on a variety $X$ we mean a nonconstant morphism from a curve with smooth irreducible components to $X$, or equivalently its image in $X$.
